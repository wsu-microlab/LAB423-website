📍 Site Introduction – RBR

Overview of the measurement site, equipment, and location.

📋 Site Information

  • Site Name RBR Station
  • Location Ross Barnett Reservoir, MS, USA
  • Latitude 32.43° N
  • Longitude 90.03° W
  • Elevation 90 m
  • Data since August 2007
  • Instruments CSAT, IRGA, basic meteorology sensors

📝 About the Site

Ross Barnett Reservoir is located in central Mississippi, USA, serving as the state’s largest drinking water resource. The reservoir is fed by Pearl River from the northeast, with a drainage area of 7,900 km2, and discharges into the south through a man-made dam and spillway. The reservoir has a surface area of about 33,000 acres (134 km2), with water depths varying from 4 to 8 m. On average, the water elevation is approximately 90.7 m above sea level in summer and 90.2 m in winter. A stationary wood platform was constructed in the south center, with a deck area of 3 m 3 m and a height of approximately 1.0 m above the water surface when initially built in August 2007. The water depth around the platform was about 5 m. A 5 m tower (Climatronics Corp.) was installed over the platform to mount most of the instruments. The distance from the tower to the shore ranged from 2 km to more than 10 km to minimize the footprint influences from surrounding lands on the water surface energy fluxes.
