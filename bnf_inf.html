📝 About the Site

The CAnopy Fluxes Experiment at Bankhead National Forest (CAFE-BNF) is a collaborative field campaign aimed at advancing understanding of turbulent exchanges of momentum, heat, water vapor, and carbon dioxide within and above a horizontally heterogeneous forest canopy. Building on the Department of Energy’s Atmospheric Radiation Measurement (ARM) AMF3 deployment site at Bankhead National Forest in northern Alabama, CAFE-BNF deploys five additional eddy covariance systems on the 140-ft main flux tower (S10), augmenting existing ARM instrumentation to create an unprecedented vertical array of eight flux systems spanning the subcanopy to the atmosphere above the canopy. This configuration enables detailed quantification of canopy coupling and decoupling processes, as well as the influence of large eddies and the roughness sublayer on canopy-atmosphere exchanges.

The observations can be integrated with high-resolution canopy-resolving simulations (WRF-LES-MCANOPY, FastEddy®) to evaluate model fidelity and identify weaknesses in representing heterogeneous canopy processes. Ultimately, CAFE-BNF will provide a mechanistic link between surface-layer exchanges, the initiation of moist thermals, and shallow convection, supporting improved parameterizations and advancing ARM’s Decadal Vision priorities on land-atmosphere coupling.
