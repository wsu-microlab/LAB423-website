Scientific motivation

Why CAFE-BNF?

Forest-atmosphere exchange

Forest canopies regulate exchanges of momentum, sensible heat, latent heat, water vapor, and carbon dioxide. CAFE-BNF observes how these exchanges vary from the subcanopy to the atmosphere above the canopy.

A distinctive vertical array

Six eddy-covariance measurement levels on the 140-ft S10 tower, complemented by a tripod EC system approximately 10 m away, provide an unusually detailed view of vertical turbulent transport.

Vertical transport

Quantify exchanges of momentum, heat, water vapor, and carbon dioxide within and above the forest canopy.

Canopy coupling

Identify canopy coupling and decoupling and examine the roles of large eddies and the roughness sublayer.

Model evaluation

Integrate observations with WRF-LES-MCANOPY and FastEddy® to evaluate representations of heterogeneous canopy processes.
